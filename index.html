A Bit About Me

I am a university student, undergraduate researcher, and engineer at heart. I enjoy all kinds of engineering project work, but I primarily design and build things I have a need to use in my daily life. My primary motivations for projects are to better understand a system or technology, as well as to challenge myself to build it cheaper or better than what is currently commercially availible, often by leveraging small-scale oppertunities not availibe to commercial manufacturing. I have built everything from a mini-fridge to a 3D printer, and I continue to use and refine these projects as I notice oppertunities for improvement.

Outside of my formal responsibilities and project work, I like to bike, camp, hike, read, and volenteer. I regularly cycle to work and classes, as this is significantlty healthier and helps me to be happier and more fufilled. There is a sense of your surroundings that cannot be gained from behind a windshield, and I much prefer to hear, feel, and smell my surroundings, as well as see them. I'd take biking in the rain over traffic any day. I also like to camp by bike, called bicycle touring. This allows me to get a similar experience to backpacking, but see much more, as I can cover over 80 miles a day by bike, vs. only 15 miles a day when hiking. Another intrest of mine is reading. I read everything that sounds interesting to me, from non-fiction tutorial and self-improvement books to fantasy and science fiction. My favorite novel of all time is Ender's Game, which I was initially introduced to when my mother's employer used it as a study on leadership qualities and effective team-building.
